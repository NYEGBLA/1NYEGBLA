    A history outlined. Date 12-10-2021

  1. 1984 T.S.A. Togobo businessman and Advisor to the awoamefiawo Srinua li and Adeladzea l dies.
  2. 1985 the church of Pentecoast violates a ban on drumming during the days leading up to the Hogbetsotso festival but unlike in the past years their drums are seized by the Traditional council in Anloga.
  3. 1986 the Asogli Traditional Area which includes the city of Ho establish the Gligbaza to promote local development and local cultural pride through the use of the Notsie story.
  4. 1987 Conflict erupted between Christians and traditional believers and their supporters in Anloga.
  5. 1954 Reconcilation efforts begin to cool animosities left in the wake of Anloga riot.
  6. 1955 united Nations plebiscite vote results in the Ewe speaking peoples of the British Togoland opting to join Goldcoast Ghana.
  7. 2007 Nyonyo Atsitsogbi Agbodo (Agbowoda) make riot and wanted to be a king Sri and kill people at Anloga.
  8. 1957 Ghana (including Anloga) gains its independence from Great Britain Ewe nationalist fevor begins to decline.
  9. 2011 the first of Gboba Srinuas Hogbetsoso I am Asiapim Urace Nyegblanua Eha l people of Anloga and their chiefs fire my house beca use how I make Gboba Chief Srinua on the stool. I was taken to court from 2004 up to 2021 now.
  10. 1450 Notsie home of the deity Mawu is wel established by this date as a regional religious center.
  11. 1881 violent confrontation between the Yewe religious order and the Christian convert Stephano Kwadzo Afelevo occurs in Woe.
  12. 1900 the first effort to bring Christianity to Anloga falls. By this date the Keta hospital is established drawing more than 2,000 patients a year from the Anlo area.
  13. 1906 Awoamefia Amedo Kpegla remembered as a taunch opponent of European influence in Anlo dies. Priests associated with two of most influential deities in Anlo ban the use of sails on the lagoon.
  14. 1907 Kofi Hayibor Kwawukume a mission educated businessman becomes the Anlo awoame mama Srinua and begins his reign by refusing to abide by many of the religlous rituals and customary in Anlo 36.
